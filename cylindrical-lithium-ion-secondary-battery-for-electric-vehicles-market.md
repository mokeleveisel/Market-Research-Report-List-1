<p><p>Cylindrical lithium-ion secondary batteries for electric vehicles consist of several critical components. Cathode materials, like lithium nickel cobalt manganese oxide, determine energy density and stability. Anode materials, primarily graphite or silicon-based, influence charge capacity and cycle life. A diaphragm, often made of polymer, separates the anode and cathode while allowing ionic transport. The electrolyte, typically a lithium salt in organic solvents, facilitates ion movement while ensuring electrochemical stability. Together, these elements enhance battery performance and longevity in electric vehicles.</p></p>
