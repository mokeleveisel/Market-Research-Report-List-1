<p><p>The non-residential occupancy sensors market consists of various types, including Passive Infrared (PIR) sensors, which detect motion by measuring changes in infrared radiation. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to identify occupancy, offering broader coverage. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine PIR and ultrasonic technologies for enhanced accuracy, reducing false activations. Other types may include microwave sensors and photoelectric sensors, each serving unique applications. Together, these technologies contribute to energy efficiency, security, and improved building automation in commercial environments.</p></p>
