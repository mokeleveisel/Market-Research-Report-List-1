<p><p>Acousto-optic modulators (AOMs) are devices that use sound waves to manipulate light, enabling control of intensity, frequency, and direction. The market for AOMs is divided into two main types: fiber-coupled and free-space. Fiber-coupled AOMs are integrated with optical fibers, ideal for compact, high-speed applications in telecommunications and data processing. Conversely, free-space AOMs are used in applications requiring direct line-of-sight light manipulation, such as in scientific research or laser systems, providing versatile handling of beam properties.</p></p>
